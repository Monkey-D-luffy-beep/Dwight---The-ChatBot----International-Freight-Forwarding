# Documentation Requirements Guide

## Overview
Proper documentation is essential for smooth international shipping and customs clearance. This guide outlines the documents typically required for various shipment types.

---

## Standard Export Documentation

### 1. Commercial Invoice
**Purpose:** Primary document for customs valuation and clearance

**Required Information:**
- Exporter and consignee details
- Description of goods
- Quantity and unit price
- Total value and currency
- Terms of sale (Incoterms)
- Country of origin
- HS (Harmonized System) codes

### 2. Packing List
**Purpose:** Details the contents and packaging of the shipment

**Required Information:**
- Number and type of packages
- Gross and net weight
- Dimensions of each package
- Contents description
- Package marks and numbers

### 3. Bill of Lading (Sea Freight)
**Purpose:** Contract of carriage and receipt of goods

**Types:**
- **Original B/L:** Required for release at destination
- **Telex Release:** Electronic release (faster)
- **Sea Waybill:** Non-negotiable document

### 4. Airway Bill (Air Freight)
**Purpose:** Contract between shipper and airline

**Information:**
- Flight details
- Shipment routing
- Weight and dimensions
- Declared value

### 5. Certificate of Origin
**Purpose:** Certifies the country where goods were manufactured

**Types:**
- **Non-Preferential CO:** General certificate
- **Preferential CO:** For FTA/trade agreement benefits

**When Required:**
- Import country regulations
- Trade agreement benefits
- Letter of Credit requirements

## Customs Clearance Documents

### Import Clearance
- Bill of Entry
- Commercial Invoice
- Packing List
- Bill of Lading / Airway Bill
- Certificate of Origin
- Import License (if applicable)
- Letter of Credit / Bank documents (if applicable)
- Product-specific certificates (as required)

### Export Clearance
- Shipping Bill
- Commercial Invoice
- Packing List
- Letter of Credit (if applicable)
- Export License (if required)
- Product-specific certificates

## Special Documentation by Cargo Type

### Hazardous Goods
- Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS)
- Dangerous Goods Declaration
- UN packaging certification
- Emergency response procedures

### Pharmaceuticals & Healthcare
- Drug Controller certificates
- Temperature records (cold chain)
- Quality certificates
- Import/Export licenses

### Food & Agricultural Products
- Phytosanitary Certificate
- Health Certificate
- Quality/Grade Certificate
- FSSAI documentation (for India)

### Machinery & Equipment
- Manufacturer's certificate
- Test certificates
- Installation instructions
- Warranty documents

### Defence & Project Cargo
- Government approvals
- End-user certificates
- Project cargo permits
- Route survey documents

## EXIM Documentation Tips

### For Exporters
1. Ensure all documents have consistent information
2. Use correct HS codes for proper duty assessment
3. Maintain copies of all signed documents
4. Pre-verify document requirements with customs
5. Use IEC (Import Export Code) on all documents

### For Importers
1. Verify documents before shipment departure
2. Ensure proper value declaration
3. Keep import licenses current
4. Pre-clear with customs when possible
5. Maintain compliance records

## Common Documentation Errors to Avoid

❌ **Mismatched information** across documents
❌ **Incorrect HS codes** leading to wrong duty
❌ **Missing signatures** or stamps
❌ **Incomplete addresses** of parties
❌ **Wrong Incoterms** usage
❌ **Expired licenses** or certificates
❌ **Undeclared hazardous** goods
