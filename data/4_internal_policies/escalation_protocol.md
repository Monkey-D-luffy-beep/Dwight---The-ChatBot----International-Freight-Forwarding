# Tiger Logistics - Escalation Protocols

## Overview
This document defines the escalation procedures for handling issues that require attention beyond standard operational processes.

**Purpose:** Ensure timely resolution of issues and maintain customer satisfaction.

---

## Escalation Levels

### Level 1: Frontline / Customer Service
**Handled by:** Customer Service Representatives

**Scope:**
- Standard inquiries and questions
- Basic shipment status updates
- Simple documentation requests
- Routine issue resolution

**Authority:**
- Answer standard questions
- Provide status updates
- Resolve minor issues
- Coordinate with operations

**When to Escalate:** If issue cannot be resolved within standard procedures or customer requests escalation.

---

### Level 2: Team Lead / Supervisor
**Handled by:** Customer Service Team Lead, Operations Supervisor

**Scope:**
- Issues unresolved at Level 1
- Customer requests for escalation
- Moderate complexity issues
- Time-sensitive matters

**Authority:**
- Approve minor service credits
- Coordinate across departments
- Contact carrier/partner management
- Adjust shipment priorities

**Response Time:** Within 2 hours during business hours

**When to Escalate:** If resolution requires department head approval or involves significant impact.

---

### Level 3: Department Manager
**Handled by:** Operations Manager, Sales Manager, Customer Service Manager

**Scope:**
- Significant customer impact
- Potential claim situations
- High-value shipment issues
- Service failure situations
- Cross-department coordination

**Authority:**
- Approve service credits up to defined limit
- Authorize expedited solutions
- Direct resource allocation
- Negotiate with partners

**Response Time:** Within 4 hours during business hours

**When to Escalate:** If requires senior management decision or has company-wide implications.

---

### Level 4: Senior Management
**Handled by:** VP Operations, Commercial Head, Director level

**Scope:**
- Critical business impact
- Major customer relationship at risk
- Significant financial exposure
- Regulatory or legal issues
- Reputation concerns

**Authority:**
- Full resolution authority
- Major commercial decisions
- Crisis management
- Strategic client retention

**Response Time:** Immediate for critical issues

---

## Issue Severity Classification

### Critical (Red)
**Characteristics:**
- Complete service failure
- Major customer event at risk
- Regulatory violation risk
- Significant financial exposure
- Media/reputation threat

**Response:** Immediate escalation to Level 3/4

### High (Orange)
**Characteristics:**
- Customer deadline at risk
- Service level breach likely
- Customer expressing strong dissatisfaction
- Potential claim situation

**Response:** Escalate to Level 2 immediately

### Medium (Yellow)
**Characteristics:**
- Issue identified but manageable
- Delay that can be mitigated
- Customer inconvenienced but not critical

**Response:** Resolve at Level 1-2, update customer proactively

### Low (Green)
**Characteristics:**
- Minor issue with minimal impact
- Information requests
- Documentation corrections

**Response:** Standard resolution at Level 1

## Escalation Triggers

### Immediate Escalation Required
- [ ] Customer explicitly requests manager/escalation
- [ ] Shipment detained by customs
- [ ] Cargo damage reported
- [ ] Significant delay (>48 hours off schedule)
- [ ] Missing or incorrect documentation affecting clearance
- [ ] Safety or security concern
- [ ] Complaint about staff conduct

### Escalate After Failed Resolution Attempt
- [ ] Issue not resolved within SLA
- [ ] Customer not satisfied with proposed solution
- [ ] Solution requires approval beyond authority level
- [ ] Pattern of recurring issues with same customer

---

## Escalation Communication

### Internal Escalation Format
When escalating internally, include:

1. **Customer Name & Account**
2. **Shipment Reference**
3. **Issue Description** (clear, factual)
4. **Timeline of Events**
5. **Actions Taken So Far**
6. **Current Status**
7. **What's Needed** (decision, resource, approval)
8. **Urgency Level**

### Customer Communication During Escalation
- Acknowledge the escalation
- Provide timeline for response
- Keep customer updated on progress
- Confirm resolution and follow-up

## Escalation Contacts

### During Business Hours
| Level | Contact Method |
|-------|----------------|
| Level 2 | Team Lead - direct contact |
| Level 3 | Department Manager - phone/email |
| Level 4 | Senior Management - through Level 3 |

### After Hours / Emergency
- Emergency contact list (maintained separately)
- On-call supervisor
- Critical issues only

---

## Resolution Documentation

### Required Documentation
For all escalated issues:
- Issue log entry
- Timeline of escalation
- Actions taken at each level
- Resolution details
- Customer confirmation
- Root cause analysis (if applicable)
- Preventive measures

### Post-Resolution Review
- Was escalation handled appropriately?
- Could issue have been prevented?
- Process improvements needed?
- Training requirements identified?

---

## Key Principles

### Do's
✅ Acknowledge the issue promptly
✅ Take ownership and follow through
✅ Keep customer informed at all stages
✅ Document everything
✅ Focus on resolution, not blame
✅ Learn from each escalation

### Don'ts
❌ Ignore or delay escalation
❌ Pass customer between departments
❌ Make promises beyond authority
❌ Argue with customer
❌ Hide information from management
❌ Close issue without customer confirmation
