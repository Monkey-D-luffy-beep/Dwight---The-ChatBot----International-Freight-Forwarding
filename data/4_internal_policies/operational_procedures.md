# Tiger Logistics - Internal Operational Procedures

## Overview
This document outlines standard operational procedures for Tiger Logistics staff. Follow these guidelines to ensure consistent, high-quality service delivery.

**Note:** This document is for internal use only.

---

## Customer Inquiry Handling

### Response Time Standards

| Channel | Initial Response | Resolution Target |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|
| Phone | Immediate | During call or callback same day |
| Email | Within 4 business hours | Within 24 hours |
| Website Form | Within 4 business hours | Within 24 hours |
| WhatsApp | Within 1 hour (during hours) | Within 4 hours |

### Inquiry Categories

1. **General Inquiries:** Route to customer service
2. **Quote Requests:** Route to sales team
3. **Shipment Status:** Handle directly or escalate to operations
4. **Complaints:** Follow escalation protocol
5. **Technical Issues (FreightJar):** Route to IT support

---

## Quote Preparation Guidelines

### Standard Quote Process
1. Gather complete shipment details
2. Verify origin and destination specifics
3. Check current rates with carriers/partners
4. Calculate all applicable charges
5. Apply standard markup/margin
6. Review for accuracy
7. Send to customer with validity period

### Required Information for Quotes
- [ ] Origin details (address, country, port/airport)
- [ ] Destination details (address, country, port/airport)
- [ ] Cargo description
- [ ] Weight and dimensions
- [ ] HS codes
- [ ] Incoterms
- [ ] Special requirements
- [ ] Preferred dates

### Quote Review Checklist
- [ ] All charges included
- [ ] Currency correct
- [ ] Validity period stated
- [ ] Terms and conditions attached
- [ ] Incoterms properly applied
- [ ] Special handling noted

## Booking Confirmation Process

### Booking Workflow
1. Receive confirmed booking from customer
2. Verify all details match quote
3. Make space booking with carrier
4. Confirm booking to customer
5. Send booking confirmation with reference number
6. Initiate documentation process

### Booking Confirmation Must Include
- Booking reference number
- Carrier and service details
- ETD/ETA
- Port/airport information
- Documentation requirements
- Customer action items

## Customer Communication Standards

### Proactive Updates
Notify customers at these milestones:
1. Booking confirmed
2. Cargo picked up / received
3. Departed origin
4. Any delays or exceptions
5. Arrived destination
6. Customs clearance complete
7. Delivered

### Communication Tone
- Professional and courteous
- Clear and concise
- Solution-oriented
- Avoid jargon when possible
- No blame or excuses

### Response Templates
Use approved templates for:
- Booking confirmations
- Status updates
- Delay notifications
- Issue resolution
- Follow-up messages

## Issue Resolution Guidelines

### Severity Levels

| Level | Description | Response Time |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| Critical | Shipment at risk, major delay | Immediate |
| High | Customer impacted, needs urgent attention | Within 2 hours |
| Medium | Issue identified, manageable | Within 4 hours |
| Low | Minor issue, no immediate impact | Within 24 hours |

### Resolution Process
1. Acknowledge the issue to customer
2. Investigate root cause
3. Identify solution options
4. Communicate plan to customer
5. Execute resolution
6. Confirm resolution with customer
7. Document for records

### Documentation Required
- Issue description
- Timeline of events
- Actions taken
- Resolution outcome
- Preventive measures

---

## Escalation Protocol

### Level 1: Customer Service
- Standard inquiries
- Basic status questions
- Simple issue resolution

### Level 2: Team Lead / Supervisor
- Escalate if:
  - Customer requests escalation
  - Issue unresolved after standard process
  - High-value shipment at risk
  - Potential claim situation

### Level 3: Department Manager
- Escalate if:
  - Significant financial impact
  - Legal/compliance concerns
  - Major customer relationship risk
  - Media/reputation implications

### Level 4: Senior Management
- Escalate if:
  - Critical business impact
  - Strategic customer affected
  - Regulatory issues
  - Crisis situations
